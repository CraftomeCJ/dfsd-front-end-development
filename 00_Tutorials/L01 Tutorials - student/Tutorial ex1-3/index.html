Top 5 Things to Do at Changi Airport

Changi Airport in Singapore is more than just a travel hub — it's a destination in itself, known for its incredible range of facilities, world-class architecture, and immersive attractions. One of the top things to do is to explore Jewel Changi, a nature-themed entertainment and retail complex connected to Terminal 1. Jewel features the world’s tallest indoor waterfall — the Rain Vortex — surrounded by a lush indoor forest, shopping outlets, and dining experiences. Another must-see is the Butterfly Garden located in Terminal 3. This is the world’s first airport butterfly garden, housing over 1,000 tropical butterflies and featuring a grotto waterfall and lush greenery — a serene escape in the middle of the airport. For passengers looking to rest, Changi provides a variety of lounges with showers, refreshments, and nap pods, as well as free snooze lounges located throughout the terminals, ideal for long layovers. If you enjoy entertainment, you can catch the latest movies for free at the theaters in Terminal 2 and Terminal 3. The airport also has entertainment zones with video games, PlayStations, and interactive activities for all ages. Finally, for a unique experience, take a dip in the rooftop swimming pool at the Aerotel Airport Transit Hotel in Terminal 1, which includes a jacuzzi and poolside bar with views of the runway. Changi Airport also offers excellent features and facilities including free Wi-Fi and charging stations, 24-hour food courts and retail stores, prayer rooms and baby care rooms, automated check-in kiosks and efficient baggage handling systems, and a Skytrain service that connects all terminals. Whether you’re just passing through or have time to explore, Changi Airport offers a wide range of experiences that redefine what an airport can be.
